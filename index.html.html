Capstone Project

The HW Dental Story

The Problem

HW Dental, a fast-growing dental chain with four Columbus-area branches (Clintonville, Polaris, Sawmill, Westerville), enrolled over 4,500 new patients in one year. Despite this growth, leadership lacked clarity on who their patients were, how they were being reached, and where marketing dollars were most effective — especially for their primary targets: Medicaid recipients and Spanish-speaking populations.

The Data Journey

  • 4,546 patients · 21 variables · real, messy data from a live practice management system
  • Standardized inconsistent city name capitalization (e.g., "columbus" vs "COLUMBUS")
  • Built a custom Medicaid indicator from raw insurance carrier names using a reference list
  • Resolved unreliable gender field by applying a multi-layer name-based estimation algorithm
  • Cleaned and categorized 73% of patients with a referral source (Google, personal referrals, insurance sites, word of mouth)

The Solution

  • Branch-level patient profiling by demographics, insurance mix, and referral source
  • Referral channel analysis to identify highest-performing acquisition pathways per location
  • Geographic mapping of patient ZIP codes to surface underserved areas and growth opportunities
  • Spanish-speaking population analysis using Language field + Spanish_Likelihood name-based estimate (≈24% of patients flagged High)
  • Communication preference segmentation to guide targeted outreach strategies

The Impact

  • Actionable recommendations for reallocating marketing spend by channel and branch
  • Identified demographic gaps between branches, informing location-specific outreach campaigns
  • Provided data-driven guidance for reaching Medicaid and Spanish-speaking communities more effectively
  • Demonstrated how name-based estimation can supplement incomplete language/demographic data in real healthcare settings
  • Delivered insights the clinic can use to make better decisions about where and how to grow
